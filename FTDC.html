Fight To Destroy Communism:

  • Creator, Developer, Programmer
  • May 2021
  • (Unity, C#)
  • Duration: 4 weeks
  • Team Size: 2

Background:

Super Mario World was the first video game I ever played. It introduced me to a parallel world filled with colors and sounds and concepts limited only by imagination. For a while Super Mario World was my favorite game.

Not too long after, I discovered that I wanted to become a game developer, and Super Mario World was a strong motivator. I never felt euphoria like I did when I first played that game, and I wanted to provide that same feeling to millions of people because life is not the same if you are not able to experience the wonder of a video game that moves you.

Hence then I yearned to create a platformer much like Mario. Once I had completed my first fully fleshed out game I had a world created through story, and I expanded on that world to create my platformer, Fight To Destroy Communism.

Learning Experiences:

Development was kickstarted using a Udemy course overviewing C# and Unity. I learned how to code in Unity using that course and the final section was to create a platformer, named TileVania. I repurposed my old sprites, produced new music, and shaped the learning experience into my own personal project.

Throughout the course I was able to review my previous C# knowledge, taking advantage of prefabs to produce flexible assets, sprite sheets to assemble animations, and input systems to give the characters life.

I dove into deeper aspects of the engine. I discovered scriptable objects, and used them to create multiple playable characters without the added space consumption. I also learned of the TileMap extension of Unity and used it effectively develop multiple platforming levels with different layers and colliders for each layer.

Conclusion:

Achieving the chore mechanics of the platformer was a satisfying achievement. I wanted to continue the project and flesh it out into a full game experience, however school restarted my free time ran thin. I therefore never considered this game out of its "Beta" version because of its lack of story and limited playability of 3 levels. However that did not retract the pride I felt in my own creation.

I took the time and energy and created a platformer with the exact same feeling of the original Mario Brothers. It felt so satisfying to jump around and slay enemies. My expanded knowledge lead me to thrive in future Unity projects and has opened the possibility for me to create a future polished platformer that can be enjoyed as much as the Mario Brothers Series.
