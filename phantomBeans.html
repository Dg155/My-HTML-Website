The Phantom Beans:

  • Creator, Developer, Programmer
  • June 2020
  • (Unity, C#)
  • Duration: 1 weeks
  • Team Size: 1

Background:

During the 2020 COVID Outbreak I was at a low in my life. I had lost the ability to see my friends and family, I couldn't finish my high school and have a graduation, and I was stuck at home without much to do. In this trying time I looked towards a life long comfort of mine, game development.

In the 10th grade I began a Udemy course that explores many aspects of the game engine Unity, and teaches you to code within C#. I made some progress and followed the provided steps to create small games like text based adventures and Atari breakout, however I never used my skills to create my own original project. I decided to revisit the course and begin once again from new!

Since I had not coded in C# for 2 years I needed a refresher, so I began a mini project that was a top down space shooter. The Udemy tutorial guided me through it under the name "Laser Defender", but after my friend had written a star wars parody I decided to theme the game around that instead. And so "Digital Wars: The Phantom Beans" had its game debut.

Learning Experiences:

With this project came the use of many basic C# characteristics. If/else statements, loops, coroutines, colliders, all of these features are necessary if you want to create anything in Unity. They are basic but yield powerful results, and provided me the foundation I needed to begin my game development journey.

I learned what a scene was within the engine, and how to string scenes together to create games. I learned about prefabs, and used them to transfer objects throughout different scenes. I even experimented with the particle systems to add pizzazz and flavor to the game.

Finally I learned the importance of transitioning from scene to scene during the gameplay. I used different technqiues such as Singletons and DontDestoryOnLoad to make the transitions smoother.

Conclusion:

It took about a week to complete the project. The Udemy tutorial guided me through most of the process, broadening my C# and Unity knowledge. I left this project with a strong foundation in Unity and was ready to tackle my next game development project.
